Spiritual Capacity

• Transcendent Orientation: "Man is a spiritual being, and is therefore capable of knowing and loving God"
• Religious Vocation: "He is called to worship God, and to serve Him in all things"
• Search for Meaning: Humans naturally seek answers to ultimate questions about existence
• Desire for Transcendence: The human heart is restless until it rests in God
"The root reason for human dignity lies in man's call to communion with God. From the very circumstance of his origin man is already invited to converse with God. For man would not exist were he not created by God's love and constantly preserved by it; and he cannot live fully according to truth unless he freely acknowledges that love and devotes himself to His Creator."

Expressions of Spiritual Capacity Across Cultures

Worship
Ritual practices, liturgy, prayer, meditation, and communal celebration
Ethical Systems
Moral codes and principles derived from religious beliefs
Sacred Art
Visual, musical, and literary expressions of transcendent reality
Mystical Experience
Direct encounters with divine reality across religious traditions
Community Formation
Religious communities organized around shared spiritual vision
Philosophical Inquiry
Rational exploration of ultimate questions and meaning
Pastoral Application:
This understanding of spiritual capacity informs the Church's approach to interreligious dialogue, recognizing that the search for God is universal. It also guides ministry to those who consider themselves "spiritual but not religious," acknowledging their authentic spiritual yearnings while inviting them to the fullness of faith.
