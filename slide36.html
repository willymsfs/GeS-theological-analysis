Faith and Culture

• Mutual Relationship: "Faith and culture are not opposed, but are called to enrich each other"
• Incarnational Principle: Just as Christ became incarnate in a specific culture, faith must be incarnated in every culture
• Cultural Diversity: The Gospel can be expressed through diverse cultural forms
• Critical Dialogue: Faith both affirms cultural values and challenges cultural elements contrary to human dignity
• Evangelization of Culture: Faith seeks to transform culture from within
"The good news of Christ constantly renews the life and culture of fallen man, it combats and removes the errors and evils resulting from the permanent allurement of sin. It never ceases to purify and elevate the morality of peoples. By riches coming from above, it makes fruitful, as it were from within, the spiritual qualities and traditions of every people and of every age."
