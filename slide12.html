Call for a New Humanism

"A new series of problems arises, calling for a new humanism, where man is defined by his responsibility to his brothers and to history."
• Responsibility-Centered: Human identity defined by responsibility rather than autonomy alone
• Relational Focus: Emphasizes relationships with others ("brothers") and with history
• Attitudinal Change: "This calls for a profound change of attitudes"
• Renewed Responsibility: Need for "a renewed sense of responsibility on the part of all"
Traditional Humanism
• Focus on individual autonomy
• Emphasis on rights and freedoms
• Often secular in orientation
• Progress through technological mastery
• Human-centered worldview
New Humanism of Gaudium et Spes
• Focus on responsibility and solidarity
• Balance of rights and duties
• Open to transcendent dimension
• Progress through ethical development
• God-centered yet fully human worldview
Contemporary Expression:
Pope Francis's concept of "integral ecology" in Laudato Si' exemplifies this new humanism by emphasizing interconnectedness between environmental, social, economic, and cultural systems, and calling for responsibility toward future generations.
