Moral Agency and Freedom

• Moral Capacity: "Man is a moral being, and is therefore capable of choosing between good and evil"
• Call to Virtue: "He is called to live a life of virtue, and to strive for holiness"
• Authentic Freedom: Freedom not as license to do anything, but as capacity for responsible self-determination
• Conscience: The inner sanctuary where God's voice echoes in human hearts
• Dignity in Choice: "Only in freedom can man direct himself toward goodness"
"Authentic freedom is an exceptional sign of the divine image within man. For God has willed that man remain 'under the control of his own decisions,' so that he can seek his Creator spontaneously, and come freely to utter and blissful perfection through loyalty to Him."
Moral Decision-Making According to Catholic Teaching
1. RECOGNIZE THE SITUATION
Identify the moral question at stake and gather relevant facts
2. FORM CONSCIENCE
Consult Scripture, Church teaching, prayer, and wise counsel
3. APPLY MORAL PRINCIPLES
Consider natural law, human dignity, common good, and specific virtues
4. DISCERN INTENTIONS
Examine motives and intended ends of possible actions
5. MAKE DECISION & ACT
Choose freely in accordance with formed conscience and accept responsibility
